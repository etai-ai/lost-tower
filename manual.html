THE LOST TOWER

Game Manual

Overview

Ancient spirits flood through a rift in the temple floor. Build elemental obelisks along the path to destroy them before they escape. Survive all 10 waves to seal the breach.

You start with 150 gold and 20 lives. Each enemy that reaches the exit costs 1 life. Reach 0 lives and the temple is lost.

Controls

Desktop

Left-click — Place the selected tower on an empty tile

Right-drag — Rotate the camera

Scroll wheel — Zoom in/out

Mobile

Tap — Place tower

Drag — Rotate camera

Pinch — Zoom in/out

HUD

♦ Gold — Earned by killing enemies. Spent to build towers.

♥ Lives — Lost when an enemy reaches the exit.

Wave button — Start the next wave. Shows wave number and enemy name.

AUTO / MANUAL — Toggle auto-start for the next wave (1.5s delay between waves).

Speed (1x / 2x / 3x) — Cycle game speed.

🔊 / 🔇 — Toggle sound on/off. All audio is synthesized — no downloads needed.

Audio & Visual Effects

Every tower type has a unique firing sound. Projectile impacts create colored particle bursts matching the tower's element. Enemies flash white when hit and explode into particles on death.

Key sound cues: a horn signals each wave start, a low drone plays on defeat, and a triumphant chord plays on victory. Enemy leaks produce a warning tone and red particles at the exit portal.

Towers

Towers can be placed on any tile that is not part of the path. Each tower fires at the closest enemy within range.

Upgrades & Selling

Click or tap an existing tower to open its info panel. From there you can upgrade or sell.

Selling

Towers can be sold for 65% of total gold invested (including all upgrade costs). This lets you correct mistakes or adapt strategy.

Tip: A Lv 3 Fire tower (100g total) deals 37.3 DPS — far more efficient than two Lv 1 towers (60g, 26.6 combined DPS). Upgrade your best-positioned towers first!

Waves

There are 10 campaign waves of increasing difficulty. Starting from wave 4, waves feature mixed enemy compositions — groups spawn sequentially, so scouts and escorts arrive before the main threat. Wave 8 is a mini-boss wave and Wave 10 is the final boss.

Strategy Guide

Early Game (Waves 1–3)

Place 2–3 Fire towers near path bends where enemies spend the most time in range. Fire towers have the best cost-to-DPS ratio early on.

Mid Game (Waves 4–7)

Add Frost towers at chokepoints to slow enemies into kill zones. Place Arcane towers where the path is dense — chain damage adds up fast against swarms.

Late Game (Waves 8–10)

The Temple Guardians (wave 8) are slow but tanky. Stack towers along the longest straight segments. For The Sealed One (wave 10), you need concentrated firepower — multiple Earth and Arcane towers with Frost support.

Endless Mode

After sealing the breach (beating wave 10), you can choose to Enter the Abyss. This unlocks an endless gauntlet of procedurally generated waves with no upper limit.

You receive +100 gold and your lives are restored to at least 10 when entering endless mode.

Endless Scaling

Each endless wave escalates in difficulty:

Enemy HP grows exponentially (~22% per wave). Speed increases gradually, capping at 5.0. Enemy count rises steadily up to 30. Rewards scale to keep upgrades viable.

Boss Waves

Every 5th endless wave is a boss wave with fewer but far tankier enemies. Every 10th wave is a mega-boss — a single colossal enemy with massive HP.

Enemy Variety

Endless waves cycle through all enemy types with escalating prefix names (Risen, Empowered, Ancient, Abyssal...). You'll face familiar enemy shapes at dramatically higher stat levels.

Tip: In endless mode, upgrading towers to Lv 3 becomes essential. Focus on a core of maxed-out Frost + Arcane towers at the best chokepoints, supported by Earth towers for raw damage. Sell underperforming Lv 1 towers to fund upgrades.

Tower Placement

Path bends are the most valuable positions — towers placed at inner corners can hit enemies on two segments. The serpentine path has many U-turns, so look for spots where 3+ path segments are within range.

Map

The map is a 20×14 grid with a serpentine path from the red portal (enemy spawn, top-left) to the green portal (exit, right edge). Towers can only be placed on non-path tiles.
